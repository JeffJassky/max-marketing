Design Notes
Hero: Total GA4 sessions as the big number. For brand clients this is the strongest digital intent signal — someone saw the brand and was curious enough to visit. For Shopify clients it's the top of the purchase funnel.

Stacked bar: 32px tall, four colored segments showing traffic source composition. Social Referral, Paid, Organic Search, and Direct. Percentages inside. Mirrors Reach Breakdown's visual pattern so the two cards feel like companions — reach is exposure, site traffic is the response to that exposure.

Channel rows: Tight, compact rows labeling each segment with session counts and directional change. Ordered by volume, largest first. Only shows sources with meaningful traffic.

Engaged sessions rate: Anchored to the right of the "Traffic Sources" title — compact, no extra row. Shows what percentage of sessions were "engaged" per GA4's definition (session lasted >10 seconds, had a conversion event, or had 2+ page views). This is the quality signal: not just "people came" but "people stayed and looked around."

Source categories: Mapped from GA4's default channel groupings. Social Referral = organic social clicks. Paid = all paid ad clicks. Organic Search = unpaid search clicks. Direct = direct URL entry, bookmarks, unattributed. Jeff can map additional GA4 channel groups as needed.

Lives on both dashboards.
